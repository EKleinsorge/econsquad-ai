The Monday AI for ED Drop — Issue #1: Your First Week with AI

May 12, 2026 4 min read

Welcome to the Monday AI for ED Drop — a weekly digest of practical AI insights, tips, and tools for economic development professionals. No hype, no fluff, just what's actually useful in your workday. Each week I'll share a few actionable ideas and a spotlight on a tool or feature worth knowing about.

Let's start at the beginning: if you're new to using AI in your ED work, the biggest mistake is trying to do everything at once. Pick one thing. Get good at it. Then expand. Here's how to set up your first week.

This Week's Tips

Tip 1

The 3 Tasks ED Pros Should Automate First

Not all ED tasks are equal for AI assistance. Start where the ROI is highest: grant narrative drafts (AI can produce a solid 70% draft in minutes — you add the community specifics), BRE follow-up emails (AI drafts a personalized follow-up based on each business's survey responses), and site selection summaries (AI synthesizes your community's data into a prospect-ready summary). These three tasks alone can save 8–12 hours per week for a typical ED professional. Master them first before expanding to other workflows.

Tip 2

How to Prompt AI Effectively for ED Work

The single biggest factor in AI output quality is prompt quality. Vague prompts get vague results. The formula that works best for ED tasks: Role + Context + Task + Format. Example: "You are an economic development grant writer. My community is [community name], a rural county of 24,000 in central Ohio with 14.2% unemployment and a growing manufacturing sector. Write the need statement section (300–400 words) for a CDBG Economic Development application focused on workforce training for advanced manufacturing. Use a direct, data-driven tone." That level of specificity consistently produces usable output on the first try.

Tip 3

Build Your Community Data Cheat Sheet

Before you can use AI effectively for your community, you need to be able to give it your community's key facts quickly. Spend 30 minutes this week building a simple reference document: population, county/city, unemployment rate, major employers (top 5), key industries, latest BLS wage medians for your primary sectors, any active incentive programs, and your elevator pitch. Paste this into every AI session as context. It will immediately improve every output you get.

Tip 4

Don't Publish Without Reading

AI tools can produce confident-sounding errors — wrong statistics, plausible-but-false claims, outdated program names. The rule is simple: everything AI drafts goes through your eyes before it goes out the door. For grant applications, verify every data point. For prospect materials, confirm every program detail. Think of AI as a very fast first-draft writer who needs a sharp editor. You're the editor. That's still your most important job.
